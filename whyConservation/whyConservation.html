Why is Rainforest Conservation Important?

Hover over the card to learn more below

Maintaining air quality and composition

Rainforests serve as carbon sinks, with the Congo Rainforest storing more than 60 billion metric tons of Carbon alone. They help offset some greenhouse gas emissions, and also serve as natural air filters.

Maintaining the water cycle

Rainforests help to regulate the flow of water, where water evaporated fro the leaves of trees and other rainforest plants contribute to cloud formation

Home to numerous species

Rainforests are home to an astounding number of species of plants, animals and more, housing nearly 80% of the worlds terrestrial biodiversity.

Support human livelihoods

Many people depend directly on rainforests for their natural resources for their livelihoods.

Medicine discovery

The biodiversity that comes from rainforests can be studied, and many medicines for common ailments are derived from compounds discovered in rainforests.

Housing and shelter

The rainforest provides shelter for many people, and several species of valuable wood comes from rainforests, which are then used for the construction of housing and furniture.

Preventing Soil erosion

Rainforests prevent soil erosion through their deep and complex root systems that dig deep into the forest floor and holds the soil in place. This helps prevent flooding and protects farmland from ruin.
